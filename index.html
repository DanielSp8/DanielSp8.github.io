I currently reside in the Kansas City area.

Our lives are often marked by the things we enjoy.

Coding is something which I've been rediscovering. When I was 10 years old, I found myself intrigued by computers. I came across a book on GWBasic, and I was struck by both the challenging aspect of it alongside the ability to create through coding.

My Father saw interest developing, and he gave me a Visual Basic suite along with its books. I dreamed of a career in the field of computer programming.

For different reasons, I got away from my passion for programming. Now, I'm rediscovering the enjoyment for coding I once studied and practiced.

A few years ago, within my heart, there was a stirring for programming taking place. I spent time researching coding boot camps, and found the price was usually outrageous. So... I attempted to study on my own. I started with the language of C, moved to C Sharp, and also read other resources such as Programmatic Programmer and Clean Code.

I also spent some time taking some Udemy courses and watching other tutorials.

There's so much to learn and much of it is so challenging! I hit some walls, backed off. I signed back up again, studied hard. I figuratively struck my head against the wall more than once.

All the while, I kept a journal of my study hours and documented what I studied.

In the midst of a desire for an enjoyable career of programming, I came across a reasonably priced boot camp called Nucamp.

I started by learning about the fundamentals of HTML and CSS, along with JavaScript.

In Nucamp's Full Stack boot camp, I learned Bootstrap. I was also pushed to learn React to build websites.

Soon after, I learned some of React Native, in order to build cross-platform mobile apps. I also had a course on backend development, which focused on NodeJS, Express and MongoDB.
